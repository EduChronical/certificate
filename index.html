5. DESCRIPTION FIELD OF THE INVENTION

This invention relates to modular prefabricated low-rise residential construction. Specifically, it encompasses zero-deep rapid-install foundation systems, seismic-resistant and waterproof geometric self-locking interlocks for chemically modified agro-waste modules, and geopolymer binder formulations for high-strength composite modules.

BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION

Conventional low-rise construction in India faces a 25% national skilled labor shortage and material limitations where raw agro-waste rots within 1–2 years. Existing modular systems exhibit monsoon water ingress and failure under Zone V seismic loads. No prior art discloses the synergistic integration of acetylated agro-waste with proprietary binders and dual-seal interlocks.

SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION

The invention provides a system comprising (a) Acetylated Agro-Waste Micro-Modules (A²WMM) with a 3.2:1 SiO₂/Al₂O₃ molar ratio binder; (b) a 1.5° Tapered Dovetail Geometric Self-Locking Interlock (GSLI) with TFE labyrinth seals and EPDM elastic ribs; and (c) a Zero-Deep Foundation Rapid Pad (ZFRP) using hot-dip galvanized ground screws.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION

Chemical acetylation replaces hydroxyl groups in lignocellulosic fibers with acetyl groups, ensuring 50-year durability. The GSLI 1.5° taper angle enables tool-free engagement with under 5kg force. Ground screws are installed using soil-specific torque (60Nm for soft soil) to ensure stability without excavation. The integrated QR-blockchain tracking provides real-time auditing of structural integrity and carbon sequestration.
